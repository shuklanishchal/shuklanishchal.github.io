Discover How it Tips.

I don't even like nonfiction books. Why is this an exception?

Multiple Stories in One

Gladwell uses a variety of different stories and anecdotes to support his point, with descriptions as good as ones in fiction books.

Easy to Read

Gladwell uses diction that even an average middle school reader can understand. He makes sure to cater to a wide variety of people through his diction, not only a group of intellectuals.

Painting with Words

The fiction-book-like descriptions paint a picture in your mind about exactly what is going on. Gladwell does not believe in leaving the readers out of any type of context, in any way.

About the Author

Malcolm Gladwell wrote The Tipping Point: How Little Things Can Make a Big Difference to give his readers a view of his idea on how epidemics spread. He outlines this idea in his book through three different “laws,” as he eloquently puts it. The main question that Gladwell tries to answer is, “How does an epidemic tip?”
