How easy is it to read, really?

It's easier than you think.


Take a quick look at this excerpt from the book:


“This was the legacy of Sesame Street: If you paid careful attention to the structure and format of your material, you could dramatically enhance stickiness. But is it possible to make a show even stickier than Sesame Street ?” (Gladwell 110).


Here’s what’s fascinating. He’s making a children’s show his focal point of explaining the Stickiness Factor, but look at the words he uses, like

  • Attention
  • Structure
  • Format
  • Material

Gladwell, through his methodical diction, is laying out his ideas in an organized, professional manner, which makes readers overlook the fact that a children’s show was just used as evidence. That's what he's really good at: using ubiquitous references and credibly explaining them with high level diction, and that's what, in fact, makes reading this book easy.

About the Author

Malcolm Gladwell wrote The Tipping Point: How Little Things Can Make a Big Difference to give his readers a view of his idea on how epidemics spread. He outlines this idea in his book through three different “laws,” as he eloquently puts it. The main question that Gladwell tries to answer is, “How does an epidemic tip?”
